This web application performs a semi-analytical calculation of the time evolution of the energy density \(\epsilon(t)\), net baryon density \(n_B(t)\), net strangeness density \(n_S(t)\), and net charge density \(n_Q(t)\) at mid-pseudorapidity produced by the primary collisions of two identical nuclei in central \((b=0\ fm)\) relativistic collisions. The web application then extracts the temperature \(T\), the baryon chemical potential \(\mu_B\), the strangeness potential \(\mu_S\), and the charge potential \(\mu_Q\) as functions of time for the created matter that is assumed to be a quark-gluon plasma in full equilibrium under the chosen statistics (quantum or Boltzmann). Five input parameters are needed for a calculation: the atomic number \(Z\) and the atomic mass number \(A\) for the projectile (and the target) nuclei, the center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\), the proper formation time of partons \(\tau_F\), and the number of timesteps to sample the time evolution. Only primary collisions between the projectile and target nuclei are considered in the calculations; therefore, the transverse expansion and secondary scatterings are neglected. The energy and net charge densities produced in such collisions are averaged over the entire transverse overlap area \(A_T=\pi R_A^2\) where \(R_A=1.12A^{1/3}\ fm\). Note: the collision energy should not be greater than 200 GeV, because our parameterization of the transverse mass rapidity density at mid-rapidity \(dm_T/dy(0)\) is based on data up to this maximum energy. A typical calculation takes about 2-3 minutes when choosing 10 timesteps to sample the time evolution, so the maximum number of timesteps is set to 100 in order to save time.

Details regarding the semi-analytical method of calculating the energy density can be found in Todd Mendenhall and Zi-Wei Lin, Calculating the initial energy density in heavy ion collisions by including the finite nuclear thickness, Physical Review C 103, 024907 (2021).
Details regarding the calculation of the conserved-charge densities and thermodynamic quantities can be found in: Todd Mendenhall and Zi-Wei Lin, Semi-analytical calculation of the trajectory of relativistic nuclear collisions in the QCD phase diagram, Physical Review C 107, 034909 (2023).
